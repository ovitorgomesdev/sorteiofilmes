Structuring Your Narrative

A great presentation tells a story. Follow this four-part structure to guide your audience from the initial problem to the final, impactful solution. This creates a clear and memorable narrative for any project.

1

The Problem

Start with the "why." Clearly articulate the user need or business challenge your project addressed. Set the stage for your solution.

2

The Process

Explain the "how." Walk through your design journey‚Äîthe research, ideation, and key decisions. Show your thinking.

3

The Insights

Reveal what you learned. What were the "aha!" moments? Highlight the key insights that shaped your final design.

4

The Impact

Finish with the "so what?" Discuss the project's outcome. Use metrics and qualitative feedback to prove its success.

Language & Quantifying Results

Using the right language is key to sounding professional and credible. This section provides phrases to help you introduce your project and describe its results, plus a visual on how to quantify your impact with data.

Functional Language

  • "The project began with the goal of addressing..."
  • "We identified a key problem where users were struggling with..."
  • "The main objective was to improve..."
  • "One of the main challenges we faced was..."
  • "To solve this, we implemented a new design that..."
  • "After several iterations, we arrived at a solution which..."
  • "As a result, we saw a significant improvement in..."
  • "The final design led to a..."
  • "User feedback confirmed that the new feature was..."
