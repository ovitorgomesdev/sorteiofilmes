New Words

  • UX (User Experience)
  • UI (User Interface)
  • User (The person who uses a product)
  • Experience (How a user feels)
  • Interface (What a user sees and clicks)
  • Design (How things are made)
  • Product (Something like an app or website)
  • Empathy (Understanding other people's feelings)
  • User-centered design (Making things for users first)
  • Portfolio (Your collection of work)

You can use online flashcards (like Quizlet) to help remember these words.

How to Speak / Grammar Tips

  • Present Simple: Use this for facts (things that are always true).
    (Example: "UX *is* about how a user uses an app.")
  • Past Simple: Use this to talk about your past journey.
    (Example: "I *started* learning design two years ago.")
  • Saying You Are Interested: Use these phrases:
    • "I'm interested in..."
    • "What excites me is..."
    • "I'm passionate about..."

Step 3: Practice with Dialogues! (Role Play) üé≠

Let's practice! One person is the **Interviewer** (asks questions), and the other is the **Candidate** (answers). Follow these examples:

Dialogue Example 1: Simple Introduction

Interviewer: Hello [Candidate's Name]. Tell me about yourself.

Candidate: Hello! My name is [Your Name]. I am a UX/UI designer with three years of experience. I am good at user research and making designs. I love creating easy-to-use apps. I started UX/UI because I like solving problems for people. I feel happy when users like my designs.

Dialogue Example 2: More Details

Interviewer: Can you tell me a little about your background and why you are interested in this UX/UI role?

Candidate: Yes, sure! I am [Your Name]. I have worked as a UX/UI designer for five years. I enjoy finding out what users need and then making designs that help them. I am good at making wireframes and prototypes. I am very interested in this job at [Company Name] because your company builds apps for education, and I am passionate about learning and making things better for students. In the future, I want to learn more about accessibility in design.

Dialogue Example 3: Focus on Skills

Interviewer: Tell me about your skills and what excites you about UX/UI design.

Candidate: My name is [Your Name], and I am a UX/UI designer with two years of experience. I specialize in creating user flows and testing designs with real users. What excites me most is the chance to make products that are not just beautiful but also very simple to use. I believe good design can help many people. I am very interested in this job because I see that [Company Name] makes products with the user first, and this is very important to me.

Speaking well and clearly is very important for a good impression. Use these tips:

Step 5: Remember Your Introduction (Memorization Tips) üß†

To speak without problems, you need to remember your introduction well:

  • üîë Remember Main Ideas, Not Every Word: Don't try to remember every single word. Just remember the main points and how you connect them.
  • üìÖ Practice Every Day (Short Breaks): Practice your introduction for a few minutes each day. Take short breaks between practice times. This helps your brain remember it for a long time.
  • Èå≤ Record Yourself Speaking: Use your phone to record your voice. Listen back. Did you speak clearly? Was your speed good?
